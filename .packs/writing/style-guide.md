# Writing style

Guidelines for the agent when helping with writing tasks. Fill in your
preferences — the agent will reference this when drafting, reviewing, or
giving feedback.

## Voice and tone

How do you want your writing to sound? Examples: conversational, academic,
technical, casual, authoritative. Any writers or publications whose style
you admire?

## Structure preferences

How do you typically organize content? Examples: short paragraphs, lots of
headings, bullet points, long-form narrative, inverted pyramid.

## Audience

Who do you write for? Examples: developers, general public, domain experts,
your future self.

## Formatting conventions

Any specific formatting rules? Examples: Oxford comma, sentence case for
headings, no passive voice, max paragraph length, footnotes vs inline links.

## Topics and themes

What do you write about? Examples: technology, productivity, fiction,
training logs, research notes.

## Things to avoid

Patterns or habits you want the agent to catch and flag. Examples: overuse
of adverbs, jargon without explanation, walls of text.
